LIFE THREATEN DISEASES AT HOME

  1. heart attack

    2. symptoms

    1. Chest pain or discomfort.
    2. Feeling weak, light-headed, or faint.
    3. Pain or discomfort in the jaw, neck, or back.
    4. Pain or discomfort in one or both arms or shoulders.
    5. Shortness of breath.

    How to decrease risk factors associated with a heart attack

    1. Stop smoking and minimize your exposure to secondhand smoke.
    2. Stay physically active daily.
    3. Control your weight if youâ€™re overweight or obese.
    4. If you have diabetes, take care by sticking to your treatment plan and managing your blood sugar.
    5. Limit your alcohol consumption.

    what to do immidiately of heart attack occur?

    1. Call 911 or your local emergency number.
    2. Chew and swallow an aspirin while waiting for emergency help.
    3. Take nitroglycerin, if prescribed.
    4. Begin CPR if the person is unconscious.

  2. diabetics

      3. symptoms

    1. confusion, dizziness, and nausea
    2. feeling hungry
    3. feeling shaky, nervous, irritable or anxious
    4. sweating, chills, and pale, clammy skin
    5. rapid heartbeat
    6. weakness and tiredness
    7. tingling in the mouth area
    8. headaches
    9. seizures
    10. coma or loss of consciousness
    11. weight loss if hypoglycemia persists

    Action to take place to reduce damage

      If the symptoms appear suddenly, the person should take a high-carb snack to resolve them, such as:

    1. a glucose tablet
    2. a sweet juice
    3. a candy
    4. a sugar lump
  3. Seizure

    4. About 1 out of 10 people may have a seizure during his or her lifetime. That means seizures are common, and one day you might need to help someone during or after a seizure.

      how to recover

    1. Stay with the person until the seizure ends and he or she is fully awake. After it ends, help the person sit in a safe place. Once they are alert and able to communicate, tell them what happened in very simple terms.
    2. Comfort the person and speak calmly.
    3. Check to see if the person is wearing a medical bracelet or other emergency information.
    4. Keep yourself and other people calm.
    5. Offer to call a taxi or another person to make sure the person gets home safely.
  4. Chronic Lower Respiratory Disease

    5. symptoms

    1. Shortness of breath.
    2. Wheezing.
    3. Increased mucus (sputum) production.
    4. Chronic cough.
    5. Racing heartbeat.
    6. Fatigue.

    treatment

    1. If you're a current smoker, the most important thing you can do to aid your treatment is to quit smoking.
    2. Exercise has also been shown to be helpful in many different lung conditions, as it helps improve lung function
    3. Whenever possible, avoid secondhand smoke and chemical fumes.
    4. Get the nutrition your body needs. Work with your doctor or dietician to create a healthy eating plan.
